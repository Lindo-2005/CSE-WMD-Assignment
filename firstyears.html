Welcome to Hostel Living

Starting life in student accommodation can be overwhelming, especially for first-year students. This guide provides practical advice to help new residents transition smoothly into hostel life.

While this guide is based on experiences from Botswana School of Business Sciences, the advice applies to students living in hostels across different campuses.

What You Are Allowed to Bring

Student hostels often have strict rules about what residents are allowed to bring. For example, at Botswana School of Business Sciences residences such as Main Campus, Windsor Inn, Block 7, BNPC, and Nicopolis, only essential appliances are permitted.

Other appliances such as stoves or sandwich makers are not allowed because the college considers them safety risks. Students are encouraged to bring personal essentials such as toiletries and cleaning supplies.

Lack of Information About Dorms

Many first-year students arrive at hostels without knowing what the environment will be like. This is especially true for off-campus residences, where limited information and photos are available online.

Common First-Year Mistakes

One of the most common challenges faced by first-year students is poor financial management, particularly spending their allowance too quickly. Because students are excited about their new freedom, they sometimes spend money on unnecessary things early in the month.

This can be a problem because first-year students often receive their allowance later than returning students while waiting for their FNB cards.

Adjusting to Independent Living

Adjusting to independent living in a hostel environment can be challenging for many students. Managing food, money, and responsibilities without parental support requires time and discipline.

Learning to budget, buy groceries, and manage time effectively is an important part of surviving the first year at college.

Tip: Always plan your spending in the first week. Many students struggle later in the month because they spend too much too early.
