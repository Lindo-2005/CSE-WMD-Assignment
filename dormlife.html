Dorm Life at BAC

Living in student accommodation is one of the biggest adjustments for many students. Students stay in different residences such as Main Campus, BNPC, Windsor Inn, Block 7, and Nicopolis.

Room and Bathroom Setup

Most rooms are shared between two to four students depending on the residence. At Windsor Inn and other off campus dorms, each room has its own private bathroom which makes things much more convenient for students compared to shared bathrooms.

Each student is responsible for the furniture and equipment in their room. If anything is damaged or goes missing, the student will be held accountable for the repair or replacement cost. Always report accidental damage to the maintenance assistant or resident counsellor immediately.

🔒 Room Rule: Always lock your room door when you leave. Do not allow other students into your room or cupboard without your permission.

Internet and Data

Students usually have access to both WiFi and BTC SIM card data provided by the school. The BTC SIM cards provide around 6GB of data per day, which is very helpful for studying, watching tutorials, or doing assignments online.

However, heavy tasks such as downloading large files or software can finish the data quickly, so students have to manage their usage carefully.

Cooking in the Dorms

Most residences do not allow cooking appliances such as stoves, hot plates, or sandwich makers because they are considered safety risks.

According to the BAC Hostel Code of Conduct, the following appliances are strictly forbidden in rooms: heaters, toasters, grillers, hotplates, electric frying pans, microwave ovens, and immersion heaters. These will be confiscated if found.

Students often get creative and use kettles to prepare simple meals. If you can find a way to cook something using hot water from a kettle, then it becomes a common survival trick for dorm life.

Laundry and Cleaning

Some residences such as Main Campus and BNPC have cleaning staff and laundry facilities. The laundry service runs every Monday and Friday between 07:00 and 14:00 at a minimum fee of P8.80 per kg for students who prefer not to do it themselves.

Bedding is collected on a block-specific schedule — Khama Block on Tuesdays, Bathoen Block on Wednesdays, and Sechele Block on Thursdays. Note that underwear and socks are not accepted for the laundry service.

Other residences like Windsor Inn and Block 7 do not have these services. Students usually wash their clothes by hand using bathtubs or sinks.

Noise and Quiet Hours

Between 22:00 and 07:30, it is strictly quiet time in all residences. No audio equipment may be played during these hours. Students are expected to refrain from talking loudly, screaming, or playing music that disturbs others at any time of the day.

⚠️ Warning: Excessive noise pollution can result in immediate expulsion from the hostel.

Rules & Conduct

The BAC Hostel Code of Conduct covers a number of important rules all residents must follow. Key points to be aware of include:

  • Smoking and drinking alcohol is not permitted inside hostel blocks
  • Possession or use of drugs will be reported to the Police immediately
  • Gambling of any kind is not allowed on campus
  • Bullying, fighting, and discrimination are strictly forbidden
  • No dangerous weapons of any kind are permitted on hostel premises
  • The main gates are locked at midnight and open again at 06:00

You can download the full BAC Hostel Code of Conduct from the Student Resources page.

Dorm Life Experience

Living in dorms helps students build friendships, learn independence, and adapt to a new lifestyle away from home. While it can be challenging at first, many students find it to be an important and memorable part of their college experience.
