Dorm Life at BAC

Living in student accommodation is one of the biggest adjustments for many students. Students stay in different residences such as Main Campus, BNPC, Windsor Inn, Block 7, and Nicopolis.

Room and Bathroom Setup

Most rooms are shared between two to four students depending on the residence. At Windsor Inn, each room has its own private bathroom which makes things much more convenient for students compared to shared bathrooms.

Internet and Data

Students usually have access to both WiFi and BTC SIM card data provided by the school. The BTC SIM cards provide around 6GB of data per day, which is very helpful for studying, watching tutorials, or doing assignments online.

However, heavy tasks such as downloading large files or software can finish the data quickly, so students have to manage their usage carefully.

Cooking in the Dorms

Most residences do not allow cooking appliances such as stoves, hot plates, or sandwich makers because they are considered safety risks.

Students often get creative and use kettles to prepare simple meals. If you can find a way to cook something using hot water from a kettle, then it becomes a common survival trick for dorm life.

Laundry and Cleaning

Some residences such as Main Campus and BNPC have cleaning staff and laundry facilities where student's sheets are washed regularly.

Other residences like Windsor Inn and Block 7 do not have these services. Students usually wash their clothes by hand using bathtubs or sinks.

Dorm Life Experience

Living in dorms helps students build friendships, learn independence, and adapt to a new lifestyle away from home. While it can be challenging at first, many students find it to be an important and memorable part of their college experience.
